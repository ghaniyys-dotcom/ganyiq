## Key Findings

### 1. Score Compression (HIGH PRIORITY)
In 3/5 videos, 8+ clips are clustered within 5 points of max score. The current scoring system doesn't create enough separation between genuinely great clips and merely good ones. This makes ranking within the elite tier arbitrary.

**Example:** In 4270Y-K2Cls, clips #1 through #8 all scored 100 or 97. The emotional weight between "father loss during career" (#4) and "starting a group together" (#8) is clearly different, but the scores don't reflect this.

### 2. Educational Content Overranking (MEDIUM PRIORITY)
Technical/educational analysis clips tend to score higher than their engagement potential warrants. In Op_zO-NkYFQ, a dry writing analysis (#3) outranked a funny relational story (#4-5).

### 3. Set-up Heavy Clips (LOW PRIORITY)
Clips that need significant context to land sometimes rank high (e.g., meeting idol story that starts mid-narrative). These work for channel subscribers but not for viral clips.

### 4. Controversy Bias (OBSERVATION)
Controversy-tagged clips consistently score 95-100. This is effective for engagement but creates a ceiling for genuine storytelling and educational content.

## Recommendations (Do Not Implement Yet)

1. **Add score spread normalization** — Force minimum 5pt gap between tiers
2. **Penalize set-up dependency** — Detect clips that start mid-story (no natural hook in first 5 seconds)
3. **Cap controversy scoring** — At 95 max unless combined with other strong signals
4. **Cross-validation** — For clips scoring 95+, enforce diverse DNA coverage
